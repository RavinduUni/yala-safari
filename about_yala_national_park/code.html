Wildlife Spotlight

Kingdom of the Leopard

Yala Block 1 boasts one of the highest densities of leopards in the world. The semi-arid thorny scrub forest provides the perfect camouflage for these elusive big cats, making Yala the premier destination for leopard sightings globally.

visibility Sighting Tips
  • Look for movement in the high branches of Palu trees.
  • Early morning (6-8 AM) is prime activity time.
  • Listen for alarm calls from deer and monkeys.

Apex Predator

Territorial Reign

The Story of Yala

From hunting grounds to hallowed sanctuary.

1900

Designated Wildlife Sanctuary

Under British rule, the area was designated a sanctuary to curb excessive hunting, marking the first step in conservation.

1938

National Park Status

Yala was officially proclaimed a National Park, cementing its legal status as a protected area for flora and fauna.

1954

Park Expansion

The park was expanded to include five blocks, significantly increasing the protected range for roaming elephants.

Today

Global Conservation Leader

Yala stands as a beacon of biodiversity, attracting researchers and nature lovers from every corner of the globe.
